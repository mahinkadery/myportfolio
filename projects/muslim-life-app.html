Muslim Life – Daily Routine & Wellness App

Helping elderly users remember important daily tasks like medicine and prayers.

Mobile App Muslim Community Wellness Reminders

Overview

Muslim Life is a dedicated mobile application designed to support the daily routines of the Muslim community, with a specific focus on elderly users. It simplifies self-care by providing an easy-to-use interface for managing medicine reminders, daily tasks, and prayer schedules, ensuring health and spiritual obligations are met without stress.

Quick Facts

  • Role: Designer & Developer
  • Users: Muslim Community (Elderly focus)
  • Platform: Mobile (iOS & Android)
  • Main Feature: Medicine Reminders

Problem & Motivation

Many older individuals within the community struggle to maintain independence due to forgetfulness, particularly when it comes to medication schedules. Missing a dose can have serious health implications, and complex apps often make the problem worse rather than better.

I wanted to build a solution that wasn't just another tech demo, but a genuinely helpful tool. My motivation was to support independence and health by creating an interface that respects their needs—simple, clear, and effective.

Core Features

Medicine Reminder System

Customizable alarms specifically designed for medication schedules, ensuring doses are never missed.

Senior-Friendly Interface

High-contrast text, large buttons, and simplified navigation to accommodate older eyes and less tech-savvy users.

Daily Routine Tracking

Simple checklists for daily tasks and habits to help maintain structure and well-being.

Care Instructions

Space for optional notes or instructions from doctors/family members attached to reminders.

User Experience & Design

The primary design philosophy was "clarity over complexity." Every screen was audited to remove unnecessary elements. Action buttons are prominent and clearly labeled.

Notifications were crafted to be gentle yet persistent, using clear language that explains exactly what needs to be done (e.g., "Time for your heart medicine") rather than generic alerts.

Impact & Learnings

  • • Community Focus: Learned the importance of designing for specific cultural and age demographics rather than a generic user base.
  • • Accessibility: Gained deep insights into accessibility standards and how vital they are for older users.
  • • Time Management: Mastered the technical challenges of handling reliable local notifications and alarms across different device states.

Future Roadmap

  • Integrating accurate prayer times based on location.
  • Family sharing features so children can monitor parents' adherence to medication.
  • Multilingual support to include native languages for better inclusivity.
  • Cloud synchronization to keep data safe across devices.
