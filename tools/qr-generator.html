What Are QR Codes?

QR (Quick Response) codes are two-dimensional barcodes that can store various types of information and be quickly scanned by smartphones and QR code readers. They were invented in 1994 by Denso Wave, a Japanese company, and have since become ubiquitous in modern digital communication.

QR codes can store significantly more data than traditional barcodes and can be scanned from any angle, making them versatile tools for sharing information.

Best Practices for QR Codes

1. Error Correction

When adding logos, use higher error correction levels (Q or H) to ensure the code remains scannable even with logo coverage.

2. Logo Guidelines

  • Use square logos with transparent backgrounds (PNG format)
  • Keep logos centered and appropriately sized (not too large)
  • Test scannability after adding logos
  • Ensure sufficient contrast between logo and QR code

3. Size Considerations

QR codes should be large enough to scan easily. Minimum recommended size is 2cm x 2cm (0.8" x 0.8") for printed materials. For digital use, ensure sufficient resolution.

4. Placement

Place QR codes in accessible locations with adequate white space around them. Avoid placing near edges or in areas that might be damaged.

5. Testing

Always test QR codes with multiple scanners and devices before finalizing. Different devices may interpret codes slightly differently.

Technical Details

Error Correction Levels

QR codes include error correction data that allows them to be scanned even if partially damaged or obscured:

  • L (Low): ~7% error correction
  • M (Medium): ~15% error correction
  • Q (Quartile): ~25% error correction (recommended for logos)
  • H (High): ~30% error correction (best for logos)

Data Capacity

QR codes can store varying amounts of data depending on the version:

  • Numeric: up to 7,089 characters
  • Alphanumeric: up to 4,296 characters
  • Binary: up to 2,953 bytes
  • Kanji: up to 1,817 characters

Download Formats

  • PNG: Raster format, best for digital use and most applications
  • SVG: Vector format, scalable without quality loss, ideal for print

Security Considerations

  • Verify URLs: Always verify URLs before scanning unknown QR codes
  • Trust Sources: Only scan QR codes from trusted sources
  • Check Destination: Check the destination URL before proceeding
  • Update Scanners: Use updated QR code scanner apps with security features
