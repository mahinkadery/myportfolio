What is a Text Comparator?

Often called a "Diff Tool" (short for difference), a text comparator is a utility that algorithmically compares two inputs to find discrepancies.

It doesn't just tell you that the texts are different; it tells you where and how. It identifies:

  • Additions: Content present in the new version but not the old.
  • Deletions: Content present in the old version but missing from the new.
  • Modifications: Lines that have been altered.

Key Features & Benefits

We use a clear, industry-standard color-coding system:

FAQs

Does it compare word-by-word or line-by-line?

Our tool primarily uses a line-by-line comparison algorithm. This is generally the most useful method for code and documents, as it preserves the context of the change. If a single word in a sentence is changed, the whole line is typically flagged as a modification (old line removed, new line added).

Is there a limit to how much text I can compare?

The only limit is your computer's memory. Modern browsers can easily handle comparing thousands of lines of text without lagging.

Can I compare files?

Currently, this is a text-based tool. You need to open your files (Word, PDF, TXT), copy the content, and paste it here. We may add direct file upload support in the future!

Tips & Best Practices
