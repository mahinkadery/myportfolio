What is a Tip Calculator?

A tip calculator (also called a "gratuity calculator" or "bill splitter") is a specialized financial utility that automates the process of calculating restaurant tips and dividing shared expenses.

It handles three main calculations:

  1. Tip Amount: Calculates the gratuity based on a percentage of the bill total.
  2. Total Per Person: Divides the bill + tip evenly among all diners.
  3. Tip Per Person: Shows how much each person is contributing to the tip.

While this math is straightforward (Tip = Bill × Percentage, then Total = Bill + Tip, then Per Person = Total ÷ People), doing it mentally while sitting at a restaurant table is error-prone and stressful. Our calculator handles it instantly and accurately.

The process is simple and takes seconds:

  1. Enter the Bill Amount: Look at your receipt and type the total into the "Bill Total ($)" field. This is typically the subtotal before tax (though you can use the post-tax amount if you prefer—the difference is usually minimal).

FAQs

How much should I tip at a restaurant?

Tipping customs vary by location, but in the United States, standard guidelines are:

  • 15-18%: Good, satisfactory service.
  • 20%: Great service. This is considered the modern standard for good service.
  • 22-25%: Exceptional service, special occasions, or fine dining.
  • Less than 15%: Only if service was noticeably poor. Consider speaking to a manager instead.

Note: Some restaurants automatically add gratuity (usually 18-20%) for parties of 6 or more. Check your receipt before adding an additional tip.

Should I tip on the pre-tax or post-tax amount?

This is a common debate. The traditional rule is to tip on the pre-tax subtotal, as tax is not a service you received—it goes to the government. However, many people tip on the post-tax amount for simplicity, and this is also widely accepted.

Our calculator works with whichever amount you enter. If your bill shows a subtotal of $100 and tax of $8 (total $108), you can:

  • Tip on $100 (traditional): Enter `100` as the bill amount.
  • Tip on $108 (simple): Enter `108` as the bill amount.

The difference is minimal (20% of $100 = $20 vs. 20% of $108 = $21.60), so choose whichever method you prefer.

How do I split a bill when people ordered different amounts?

Our calculator assumes an even split (everyone pays the same amount). If people ordered different items:

  • Option 1 (Fair but Complex): Each person calculates their own portion + tip separately.
  • Option 2 (Simple but Rough): Use our calculator to see the "per person" amount as a baseline, then let people adjust based on what they ordered.
  • Option 3 (Common Practice): Split evenly anyway. The convenience often outweighs the small cost difference.

Can I use this for delivery or takeout orders?

Yes! For delivery services (DoorDash, Uber Eats, etc.), tipping is usually done through the app. However, if you're picking up food yourself and want to tip the staff:

  • 10-15% is common for takeout/counter service.
  • Enter your order total and desired tip percentage in our calculator.

Does the calculator round the results?

We show results to two decimal places (the standard for currency). You can choose to round up when writing the tip on the receipt (e.g., if the calculator shows $18.47, you might write $19.00 to make it a round number).
