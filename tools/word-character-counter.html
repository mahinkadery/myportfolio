What is a Word & Character Counter?

A Word & Character Counter is an online tool that analyzes a body of text and provides statistical data about its length and composition. While standard word processors (like Microsoft Word or Google Docs) have this feature built-in, they can be slow to load or require an account. An online counter offers immediate access for quick checks.

This tool is essential for anyone working with text constraints. Different platforms count "length" in different ways. Some care about the number of words (academic papers, blog posts), while others care strictly about the number of characters (SMS, Twitter/X, database fields). Our tool breaks down these metrics simultaneously, ensuring you have all the data you need at a glance.

FAQs

Does the character count include spaces?

Yes, the main "Characters" metric includes all spaces, tabs, and punctuation. This is the standard for most platform limits. However, we also provide a "No Spaces" count for specialized needs.

Is there a limit on how much text I can check?

There is no hard limit imposed by our tool. You can paste entire book chapters or long reports. The only limitation is your browser's memory, but modern devices can handle hundreds of thousands of words without issue.

Do you save my text?

Absolutely not. We operate with a strict privacy-first policy. Your text is processed locally in your browser's temporary memory. Once you close or refresh the tab, the data is vanished. We do not store, log, or analyze your content.

Does this work on mobile?

Yes! ToolfolioHub is fully responsive. You can use this tool on your iPhone, Android, or tablet just as easily as on a desktop. It is perfect for checking caption lengths on the go.

Why is the word count different from Microsoft Word?

Word counting algorithms can vary slightly. Some count "hyphenated-words" as one, while others might count them as two. Our tool uses a standard web-based algorithm that aligns with how most online platforms count words, ensuring accuracy for digital publishing.
